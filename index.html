About Me

Hi, I'm Jessica! I am a robotics engineer who loves building systems that move with intention. I specialize in simulation, control, and mechanical prototyping. I earned my M.S. in Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering from UCLA and my B.S.E. in Mechanical Engineering from Duke University. I currently contribute to the UCLA Robotics and Mechanisms Lab, where I work on humanoid motion validation and safety through simulation. My experience spans from soft robotic actuators to quadruped locomotion and industrial robot simulation. Take a look at some of my experience below!

Contact Modeling Parallel End-Effector

Developed a 2D physics-based simulation of a soft robotic gripper grasping fruit using elastic beams and contact modeling

  • Implemented the Modified Mass Method to simulate accurate contact dynamics between flexible gripper prongs and a rigid fruit model

  • Modeled elastic beams with tension, compression, and torsion springs to represent soft prongs; tested on flat and curved (circular) fruit surfaces

  • Analyzed reaction forces and identified critical contact nodes to guide mechanical design decisions and minimize fruit damage

  • Explored effects of fruit radius and gripper material stiffness (Young’s modulus) on force profiles and deformation behavior

  • Designed the simulation framework to support customization for gripper geometry, material properties, object shapes, and boundary conditions

Magnetic Levitation Device

Designed and implemented a PID-controlled magnetic levitation system to stabilize and levitate objects using electromagnetic force

  • Derived a full nonlinear model of the MagLev plant from first principles, including photo-sensor behavior, electromagnetic force, and dynamic equations

  • Linearized the system and developed a second-order transfer function using Laplace transforms for controller design and stability analysis

  • Utilized MATLAB for system simulation and PID controller tuning; applied Ziegler-Nichols method when initial values were too aggressive

  • Tuned PID parameters to achieve low overshoot, fast settling time, and robust levitation under varying inputs (step, sine, square, random)

  • Demonstrated controller robustness by levitating multiple object types, including non-spherical loads such as a paper hat and plastic cup

Candle Wick Detection

Developed an Open-CV based candle wick detection pipeline to use with the candle lighting robot.

  • Live webcam images are processed with a color mask to detect a specified color

  • The centroid of the largest region of the desired color is found, and is used to identify the wick location

  • Intrinsic and extrinsic camera properties are used to calculate the coordinates of the candle wick with respect to the robot base

PWM Controller

Designed and built a PWM-based motor speed controller using a 555 timer and MOSFET switching circuit

  • Implemented a DC-DC converter circuit where a 555 timer generates PWM pulses to regulate motor voltage via a MOSFET switch

  • Enabled dynamic speed control by adjusting the duty cycle of the PWM signal with a potentiometer, varying the output voltage from 0 to the input voltage

  • Analyzed the impact of duty cycle modulation on motor performance and verified linear speed-voltage correlation through oscilloscope traces

  • Optimized circuit efficiency and reduced component stress using high-frequency switching, extending motor and system lifespan

  • Demonstrated a cost-effective method of motor control suitable for small robotics and electronics projects using only passive components and a 555 IC
