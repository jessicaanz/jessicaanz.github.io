About Me

I am a robotics engineer passionate about creating systems that move with intention. I specialize in simulation, control, and mechanical prototyping. I earned my M.S. in Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering from UCLA and my B.S.E. in Mechanical Engineering from Duke University. I currently contribute to the UCLA Robotics and Mechanisms Lab, where I work on humanoid motion validation and safety through simulation. My experience spans from soft robotic actuators to quadruped locomotion and industrial robot simulation, always driven by a curiosity for how design, software, and control come together in physical systems.
